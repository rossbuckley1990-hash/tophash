THE PROBLEM

Structure is the dark matter of data — everywhere, unseen, unindexed.

Modern data infrastructure hashes bytes, embeds text, and indexes rows. But 80% of enterprise data — software dependency graphs, molecular scaffolds, transaction networks, model architectures — is fundamentally structural. None of it has a native identity primitive. None of it is searchable by shape. None of it is provably attestable.

SHA-256 hashes bytes.  Vectors embed text.  Nothing fingerprints structure.
